a.k.a. Panorama

Basically

The affine transformation works well when two conditions are satisfied:
1. When the panorama is really far away, because when the view is really close, the stretch would be too big for sift to bear. This is demonstrated in Fail Case A.
2. When the panorama images have many detailed crossovers, as many and as humanly indistinguishable as possible. The opposite, i.e. do not have enough common points, can be caused in two ways, either the two images just don't overlap that much, or that they overlap at areas that are essentially homogenous, or have the same colour, which is demonstrated in Fail Case B.


Most of the images used in this report utilised either bilinear interpolation or nearest neighbour
 Full sets of test images are in the testimgs folder in hw5 directory
